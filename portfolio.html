Hi, I'm Nishant.

I believe every hard thing is the start of something new, and worth running toward. I've taken that to heart.

I like creating things, and figuring out the business around them. I helped build an olfactory-integrated VR project, a couple of AI tools, and picked up finance at a few investment firms. None of it was perfect, but I learned a lot. A product only matters if it reaches someone, so I learned to pitch and sell it too.

The rest comes from service: foster-youth advocacy, leading my student body, and a lot of nights on stage. But before any of that, I'm a son, the oldest of three brothers, and a friend to the people I care about. Nothing I've made happened alone. The best of it always came from working with others.

Who's behind it.

The awards are the easy part to list. This part actually explains them: how I think, what I value, and who I am when the projects are set aside.

01

Collaboration

Nothing I've made happened alone. I'll take a team that rises together over winner-take-all every time. The best of my work always came from other people.

02

Intellectual curiosity

I learn things no one assigned me: the physics behind a fusion essay, finance at real investment firms, AI, a dozen emerging fields. I'm curious for its own sake.

03

A drive to help others

The work that means the most to me starts with who it's hurting: foster youth the system forgets, patients who can't leave a hospital room.

04

A drive to solve problems

When I notice a problem, I build the thing that fixes it, then do whatever it takes to get it to the people who need it.

05

An obsession with innovation & technology

I'm pulled toward what's next: VR, AI, space, MedTech. I'd rather build the future than read about it.

06

An artist at heart

Twenty-two stage productions, a screenplay, a camera I rarely put down. Art is how I learned to read a room, sit with discomfort, and find the human in a problem before I try to solve it. I think it's what keeps my engineering honest.

What I build, and why

Problems worth solving.

Every one of these started the same way: a problem I couldn't stop thinking about, usually because of who it was hurting. I build the thing, then figure out how to get it to the people who need it.
